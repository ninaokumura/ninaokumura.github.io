Nina's learning journey

Identity

For this challenge I had to do a lot of reflection about myself and about how others see me. It is not always easy to put into words who you are and what defines you.

To begin with, I think what really defines my identity is my cultural background. I was born and raised in Brazil but my grandparents,from both sides, are from Japan. So, I guess I built my personality from that.

And just so you know, Brazil is home to the largest Japanese community outside of Japan. At the time, japanese people were trying to flee the Second War and that's how my grandparents ended up in Rio de Janeiro.

Values and Strengths

Regarding the construction of my values and strengths, my family played a very important role. They were responsible to teach me most of my values, such as, respect, empathy, discipline and gratitude.

My parents are buddhists and some values I also got from there. Buddhists believes in Karma, which basically means that every action has a consequence. So do good and good will come to you.

That all being said, I consider myself a honest, patient and organized person. On the other hand I am not a confident and an extroverted person, so I tend to feel easily overwhelmed and that could possibly be an obstacle in regards my learning.

I truly hope my values and strengths can help me overcome my limitations throughout this course.
