Neuroplasticity

Our brain is not a static organ of our body, it is in fact very dynamic and powerful. It can grow and create new neurons connections everyday. Neuroplasticity is the science behind all that, and in simple terms, it means that our brain is capable to adapt and develop new skills and knowledge through effort and practice.

Carol Dweck, an American psychologist, made an experiment to understand how the principle of neuroplasticity can benefits people of all ages. According to her studies there is two types of mindsets, the fixed and the growth mindset.

Fixed Mindset VS. Growth Mindset

In a fixed mindset human traits like intelligence and personality are seen as something you cannot change. This mindset can lead you to an extreme fear of failure and as a consequence, it will make you avoid challenges.

People with a fixed mindset feel as if they have no control over their abilities, and simply do not know how to behave when facing difficulties. This thoughts can make you develop low self-esteem.

On the other hand, people with a growth mindset believes that you can improve and develop new skills if you put effort and keep practicing everyday. It is hard to believe, but according to Carol’s experiment she proved that the students who knew that our brain is constantly growing and its capable to learn and adapt; had a better performance at school when compared with the students who doesn't know anything about neuroplasticity.
