Emotional Intelligence

Emotional intelligence is the ability to recognize and understand not only your own emotions but also help you perceive how the others around you are feeling as well. It is a good practice to better manage these emotions in order to overcome future challenges.

Dan Goleman is a psychologist who was responsible for turning the concept of emotional intelligence popular with his book Emotional Intelligence in 1996.

According to Goleman there are 5 component's that help you improve your emotional intelligence:

Self awareness

Self awareness is all about learning to better understand your emotions and thoughts and what actions you can take to better manage these emotions and the effect they have on you.

Self regulation

Self regulation is about staying in control of the situation. You need first understand who you are and how do you behave when facing a challenge. This will help you regulate reactions to strong emotions, such as, anger and frustration.

Motivations

Self motivation is definitely not an easy skill to put in practice. So, specially if you are learning something new by yourself you gotta need some guidance to help you stay motivated.

For me, what really helps me stay motivated is to keep my goals clear. So this way I can kind of "trick" my brain to believe that even though the path is hard I will eventually achieve my goals if I don't give up.

Empathy

Empathy is the ability to understand the other people's emotions and thoughts. This skill helps us build strong relationships once we can truly understand the angsts of those around us.

Social skills

In order to improve your social skills you need to reflect on how do you usually resolve the conflicts in your life, how can you motivate others around you and also how can you improve your communication skills when interacting with someone.

EQ VS IQ

IQ is the intelligence quotient and measures your ability to use logic to solve a problem. It is related to the rational part of your brain.

IQ is an important skill to have but the experts in this field recognize that EQ should have more attention because it can interfere in your social interactions and your daily routine.
